Introduction

  • This app describes the properties of an exponential distribution.
  • The user inputs the rate parameter \(\lambda\) and the app draws samples from the exponential distribution and plots the histogram.
  • The app also calculates the distribution of the mean of samples drawn from the exponential distribution
  • Note: This is different from the taking a large number of draws from the exponential distribution (as shown in the plot in the next slide which looks exponential).

Exponential distribution

A random variable is said to follow an exponential distribution with rate \(\lambda\) is the associated probablity density is \[P(x) = \lambda \exp(-\lambda x)\] and it is defined for \(x>0\). It describes the times between events in a Poisson process i.e., a process in which the events occur continuously and independently at a constant average rate \(\lambda\).

If \(X\) is a random variable with this density, then the expected values of the mean and standard deviation are both \(1/\lambda\).
